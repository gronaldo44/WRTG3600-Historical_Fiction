Kaiser Wilhelm Institute - Hans Nachtsheim

Hans Nachtsheim was never prosecuted and died peacefully in 1979.

Served as head of the Experimental Hereditary Pathology department at the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Anthropology, Human Heredity and Eugenics (KWI-A) from 1941 to 1945. He was elevated as acting director of KWI-A in 1943.

Hans Nachtsheim`s work for KWI-A involved exploring hereditary diseases in animals. He would try to replicate his experiments` findings on animals in children from the Aktion T4 euthanasia program. Though none of the children have been found to have died directly from his experiments, it is notable that of the children known to be experimented on by Nachtsheim, only one survived the Nazi regime.

Notable Achievements and Honorifics

  • 1921
    Associate professor at the Agricultural University in Berlin and head of the Institute for Hereditary Research.
  • 1941
    Head of the Experimental Hereditary Pathology department at Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Anthropology (KWI-A).
  • 1943
    Acting director of Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Anthropology (KWI-A).
  • 1946
    Served as professor of genetics and director of the Institute for Genetics at Berlin University.
  • 1949
    Moved to newly established Freie Universität Berlin, where he held a chair in Biology and Genetics, and founded its Institute for Genetics.
  • Post-1945 (Exact date unknown)
    Decorated with the Großes Verdienstkreuz mit Stern (Knight / Grand Cross of the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany).
  • 1970
    For his 80th birthday, Freie Universität honored Hans Nachtsheim as a Vererbungswissenschaftler von hohem Rang (geneticist of high rank).
